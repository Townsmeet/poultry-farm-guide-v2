Coccidiosis is a common parasitic **ailment** that attacks chickens, transmitted through protozoan parasites of the genus Eimeria.
Chickens contaminated with coccidiosis can show signs such as [diarrhea,](/blog/How-to-stop-diarrhea-in-chickens/) weight loss, lowered egg production, and even death
. While traditional remedies for coccidiosis contain drugs such as amprolium and sulfadimethoxine, some chicken keepers use organic remedies to control the disease.
This article will explore some natural treatments for coccidiosis in chickens.
Before that, let's quickly look at some signs / preventive measures against coccidiosis:
Below are some signs of coccidiosis in chickens
## **Symptoms Of Coccidiosis in chickens**
Below are some signs of coccidiosis in chickens
**![signs of coccidiosis](https://farm-assets.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/cocci+signs+2.jpg)**
-   Bloody diarrhea
-   Loss of appetite
-   Weight loss
-   Droopiness or lethargy
-   Decreased egg production
-   Huddling or piling together
-   Pale comb or wattles
-   Dehydration
-   Ruffled feathers
-   Reduced activity
It's important to note that some of these symptoms may also be present in other [poultry diseases](/blog/12-common-diseases-of-layer-chickens-causes-symptoms-and-treatment/) or conditions, so it's crucial to consult with a veterinarian or [poultry specialist](/about/) for a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.

## **Preventive measures for coccidiosis:**
#####  Here are some preventive measures for coccidiosis:
-   Keep the poultry coop clean and dry
-   Provide fresh water and feed
-   Practice top biosecurity measures, such as limiting contact between chickens from external flocks
-
    Avoid overcrowding chickens
-
    Use probiotics to promote intestine fitness and immunity
-
    Avoid feeding chickens contaminated feed or water
-
    Provide enough airflow in the poultry coop to forestall
    moisture build-up
-
    Quarantine new chickens earlier before introducing them to the flock to prevent the spread of diseases
-
    Monitor chickens  for symptoms of sickness and isolate any ailing birds promptly
-
    Use herbal remedies, such as herbs and garlic, to help prevent and deal with coccidiosis naturally.

Apple cider vinegar is a famous herbal cure for coccidiosis in chickens. It includes acetic acid, which has antibacterial and antifungal residences that can assist forestall the increase of coccidia in the chicken's gut.

To use apple cider vinegar as a herbal cure for coccidiosis in chickens, add 1-2 tablespoons of raw, unfiltered apple cider vinegar

## 5\. **Probiotics**
**![Fermented chicken feed is a good source of probiotics](https://farm-assets.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/chicks+eating+fermented+feed.jpg)**
> **Fermented feed is a good source of probiotics**
Probiotics are every other herbal therapy for coccidiosis in chickens that can assist to control the disease.
Probiotics are [recommended microorganisms](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0044848617301876\)) that can assist to preserve a wholesome intestine environment, which is vital for stopping the increase of hazardous microorganisms and parasites such as coccidia.
To use probiotics as a herbal remedy for coccidiosis in chickens, you can add probiotic dietary supplements to the chicken's consuming water or feed.
Look for probiotic dietary supplements that are specially designed for chickens and contain useful bacteria such as Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium.
![Probiotic products/supplement](https://farm-assets.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/probiotic+1.jpg)
Probiotics supplement
In addition to supplements, you can additionally give probiotics to chickens through fermented ingredients such as *kefir* or yogurt. These meals contain cultures of helpful microorganisms that can assist to enhance the chicken's intestine health.
It's vital to note that probiotics need to be used as a preventative measure instead than as a therapy for a lively coccidiosis infection.
Probiotics can help to maintain a healthy intestine environment, which can help to stop coccidiosis from developing in the first place. However, as soon as the hen has a serious infection, probiotics on their own may not be adequate to deal with the disease.
